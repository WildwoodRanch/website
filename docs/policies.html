POLICIES

VACCINATION POLICY

It is not required for campers or staff to be vaccinated against FLU, COVID-19, or major childhood illnesses, but it is highly recommended.

ILLNESSES POLICY

Do not send campers to Wildwood Ranch who have symptoms of FLU, COVID-19, or other communicable diseases. If campers develop symptoms while at camp they will be isolated and provided a mask to wear until their adult comes to pick them up. These symptoms include new-onset of a cough, sore throat, diarrhea, fever, vomiting, loss of taste, and all others listed here You will be notified if a camper or staff in close contact to your camper (in your camper's group) tests positive for COVID-19. When a camper/staff has symptoms of or tests positive for COVID-19, they must isolate and not attend camp for at least 5 days. Campers/staff may return to Wildwood Ranch in days 6-10 if their symptoms have resolved, there has been no fever for at least 24hrs with no fever-reducing medicines, and they wear a tight-fitting mask (kn-95 or better) at all times until they test negative. After day 10 they do not need to wear a mask unless they want to.

MASK POLICY

Masks are optional in all areas at Wildwood Ranch. Campers and staff are welcome to wear masks if they prefer and will not be discriminated against. The exception is that a well-fitted, kn95 or better, mask must be worn in days 5-10 post COVID infection if campers or staff are returning to Wildwood Ranch.

REFUND POLICY

If sessions are canceled at least two (2) weeks in advance of the starting date of the session, tuition will be refunded less 10%. If sessions are canceled within two (2) weeks of the starting date of the session, there is no refund. Should your camper not be able to attend due to personal or family illness, we will do our best to accommodate them in another session. Cancellations can be arranged by email or phone call to Wildwood Ranch.

BEHAVIOR POLICY

Campers are expected to follow basic safety rules while at Wildwood Ranch. This includes treating all animals and other campers, CITs, staff, and volunteers with dignity and respect. Bullying and violence will not be tolerated. We will do our best to help campers adjust their behaviors and have a great week at camp, often with the help of caregivers, but we reserve the right to send campers home for the day or duration of the week without a refund if repeated incidents occur that endanger themselves, others, animals, or camp propety. We usually welcome campers to join us in the following summer after being sent home from camp since a tremendous amount of personal growth occurs in a year for young kids!

Our rules include entering the horse paddocks only while accompanied by a staff member. Wearing closed-toe shoes, comfortable clothes to protect against chaffing (no swimsuits without coverups) and a helmet while riding horses. Dressing appropiately for farm and outdoor activites so that campers stay comfortable and are okay to get dirty. Following instructions for all activites so that we stay safe at all times. Staying with your group and notifying the counselor before heading to the restroom or other location so that all campers remain accounted for.

CELL PHONE and TECHNOLOGY POLICY

We are a farm-based day camp that spends our days outside. In our mission to foster connections and help campers learn to intentionally make community, we do not allow phone usage at camp. Campers may have their phones in their backpack if it makes your feel safer, but we will not allow them to be used during camp. Same goes for smart watches. Campers may wear them but if found using them at camp we will request that they remove them and ask you to keep it at home for the remainder of the week. We understand that phones and technology are highly integrated into the lives of children today, and we see nothing inherently wrong with that. But we do wish to be a safe place where campers can step away from technology to see the people, animals, and nature right in front of them. To learn to lean in to our activities and try something new. To know what it feels like to have a moment with nothing to do and enjoy it. We appreciate your cooperation and support in this outlook and policy!
